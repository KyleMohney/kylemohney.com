Recruiter FAQ

Q: Tell me about a time you solved a difficult problem under pressure.

At Thumbtack, my primary role was handling escalations and documentation. But when the platform faced compliance concerns due to outstanding unresolved negative reviews, leadership turned to me — the company's go-to cross-department problem-solver — even though no system for this existed. I partnered with our legal team to draft a library of approved templates covering a range of issues. I then onboarded Thumbtack to Sprout Social, where I built a custom system of tags and sorting to auto-filter reviews and produce accurate sentiment and root cause reporting. Using Sprout's tools, I generated monthly sentiment reports and used them to match the right template to each review. The result? I cleared the entire backlog by responding to over 2000 reviews, reversed the negative trend, and left behind a scalable framework that an entire team could adopt for ongoing maintenance.

Q: What's a project you're proud of?

Building an AI-assisted public review workflow that saved Thumbtack's App Store presence. By integrating sentiment tagging with operational scripts, we responded to over 2,000 reviews in record time and reversed a negative trend that threatened our mobile ranking.

Q: How do you approach integrating AI into your work?

I treat AI as a force multiplier — a way to amplify human judgment and creativity. My goal isn't to replace people, but to give them sharper tools and faster feedback so they can focus on high-value work.

Q: How do you keep your skills sharp?

I split my time between hands-on technical projects and disciplines that sharpen focus and timing, like Historical European Martial Arts. On the tech side, I build AI-powered tools, refine game engines, and experiment with workflow automation. On the physical side, I train with sword and grappling to keep my mind sharp and adaptive.

Q: Tell me something unique about you.

I'm a trophy-winning rapier fencer and lifelong musician. I've played in Nashville, collaborated with major artists, and even performed acoustic sets with my wife. Those experiences taught me rhythm, connection, and how to engage an audience — skills I bring to my professional work as well.
